Troubleshooting

Common Refrigerator Problems & Fixes

Refrigerators work continuously, and over time certain components can wear out. Below are the most common refrigerator issues and the parts that typically resolve them.

Refrigerator Not Cooling

When your refrigerator fails to cool properly, food spoilage can occur. This issue is commonly caused by airflow or cooling system failures.

  • • Failed compressor
  • • Defective evaporator or condenser fan
  • • Faulty thermostat or sensor

Recommended parts: Compressor, fan motor, thermostat

Excessive Frost or Ice Buildup

Ice buildup inside the freezer or refrigerator compartment is usually related to defrost system problems.

  • • Failed defrost heater
  • • Malfunctioning defrost thermostat
  • • Defrost timer or control board failure

Recommended parts: Defrost heater, defrost thermostat, control board

Water Leaking Inside or Outside

Water leaks can damage floors and internal components. This issue is often caused by blocked or damaged water systems.

  • • Clogged defrost drain
  • • Cracked water line
  • • Faulty water inlet valve

Recommended parts: Drain tube, water line, inlet valve

Loud or Unusual Noises

Buzzing, clicking, or rattling noises usually indicate worn or failing mechanical components.

  • • Worn fan motor bearings
  • • Loose internal components
  • • Compressor starting issues

Recommended parts: Fan motor, mounting hardware, compressor relay
