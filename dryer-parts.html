Troubleshooting

Common Dryer Problems

When a dryer fails to heat, tumble, or complete cycles properly, drying performance drops and energy usage increases. These are the most common dryer issues and their typical fixes.

Dryer Not Heating

Clothes taking too long to dry or remaining damp usually indicates a heating system failure.

  • • Burnt heating element
  • • Blown thermal fuse
  • • Faulty thermostat

Common replacements: Heating element, thermal fuse, thermostat

Drum Not Spinning

A dryer that runs but doesn’t tumble often has a mechanical drive issue.

  • • Broken drive belt
  • • Worn drum rollers
  • • Faulty motor

Common replacements: Drive belt, drum roller, motor

Dryer Making Loud Noises

Squealing, thumping, or grinding sounds usually point to worn internal components.

  • • Worn drum rollers or bearings
  • • Damaged idler pulley
  • • Loose internal hardware

Common replacements: Drum rollers, idler pulley, bearings

Dryer Won’t Start

When the dryer won’t power on, the issue is usually electrical or safety-related.

  • • Defective door switch
  • • Failed start switch
  • • Control board malfunction

Common replacements: Door switch, start switch, control board
