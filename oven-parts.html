Troubleshooting

Common Oven Problems

When an oven doesn’t perform correctly, cooking results suffer. Below are the most frequent oven issues and the components that usually resolve them.

Oven Not Heating Properly

If the oven fails to reach or maintain temperature, cooking times become inconsistent.

  • • Failed bake or broil element
  • • Faulty temperature sensor
  • • Control board malfunction

Common replacements: Heating element, sensor, control board

Uneven Cooking or Hot Spots

Food cooking unevenly often points to airflow or temperature regulation issues inside the oven.

  • • Damaged convection fan
  • • Malfunctioning thermostat
  • • Heat loss from worn door seal

Common replacements: Convection fan, thermostat, door gasket

Gas Oven Won’t Ignite

When gas ovens fail to ignite, it usually relates to ignition or gas flow components.

  • • Faulty igniter
  • • Defective gas valve
  • • Electrical connection issues

Common replacements: Igniter, gas valve

Oven Door Not Closing Properly

Heat escaping from a poorly sealed door affects temperature accuracy and energy efficiency.

  • • Worn door hinges
  • • Damaged gasket or seal
  • • Loose mounting hardware

Common replacements: Door hinges, gasket
