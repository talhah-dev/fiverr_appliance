Troubleshooting

Common Washer Problems & Solutions

Washing machines can develop issues over time. Below are the most common washer problems and the replacement parts that typically solve them.

Washer Not Draining

If your washer won’t drain properly, water may remain in the drum after a cycle finishes. This issue is often caused by a failed drain pump or a clogged hose.

  • • Faulty drain pump
  • • Clogged or damaged drain hose
  • • Blocked filter or coin trap

Recommended parts: Drain pump, drain hose, pump filter

Loud Noise or Excessive Vibration

Unusual noises or violent shaking during spin cycles usually indicate worn mechanical components.

  • • Worn drum bearings
  • • Damaged suspension rods
  • • Broken shock absorbers

Recommended parts: Bearings, suspension rods, shock absorbers

Washer Won’t Start

When a washer refuses to start, the issue is often electrical or related to safety mechanisms.

  • • Defective control board
  • • Faulty lid or door switch
  • • Power supply issues

Recommended parts: Control board, lid switch, wiring harness

Water Temperature Issues

If your washer isn’t heating water correctly or only fills with cold water, the issue is usually related to sensors or inlet valves.

  • • Failed temperature sensor
  • • Defective water inlet valve
  • • Control board malfunction

Recommended parts: Temperature sensor, inlet valve, control board
