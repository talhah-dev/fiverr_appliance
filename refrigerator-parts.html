Troubleshooting

Common Refrigerator Problems

Refrigerators operate continuously, and certain components may wear over time. Below are frequent issues homeowners face and the parts that typically resolve them.

Refrigerator Not Cooling Properly

When cooling performance drops, food safety and freshness are affected. This problem is commonly related to airflow or cooling system components.

  • • Failed compressor
  • • Defective condenser or evaporator fan
  • • Faulty thermostat or temperature sensor

Common replacements: Compressor, fan motor, thermostat

Ice or Frost Buildup

Excessive frost inside the freezer or refrigerator section usually indicates a failure in the defrost system.

  • • Defrost heater failure
  • • Defrost thermostat malfunction
  • • Control board or timer issues

Common replacements: Defrost heater, thermostat, control board

Water Leaking From Refrigerator

Water leaks can damage flooring and internal components and are often caused by drainage or water supply problems.

  • • Blocked defrost drain
  • • Cracked or loose water line
  • • Faulty water inlet valve

Common replacements: Drain tube, water line, inlet valve

Unusual Noises During Operation

Buzzing, clicking, or rattling sounds typically point to worn mechanical or electrical components inside the unit.

  • • Worn fan motor bearings
  • • Loose internal hardware
  • • Compressor relay issues

Common replacements: Fan motor, mounting hardware, start relay
