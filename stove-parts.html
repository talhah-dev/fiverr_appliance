Troubleshooting

Common Stove Problems

Stove performance issues can affect cooking quality and safety. Below are the most common stove problems and the parts that usually resolve them.

Burner Not Heating Properly

Uneven or weak heat output is often caused by damaged burners, faulty heating elements, or poor electrical connections.

  • • Worn burner or heating element
  • • Loose wiring connections
  • • Defective control switch

Common replacements: Burner, heating element, control switch

Gas Burner Won’t Ignite

When gas burners fail to ignite, the issue is usually related to ignition or gas flow components.

  • • Faulty igniter or spark module
  • • Clogged burner ports
  • • Defective gas valve

Common replacements: Igniter, spark module, gas valve

Inconsistent Flame or Heat

Fluctuating flame size or heat output can make cooking difficult and may indicate regulator or burner issues.

  • • Faulty pressure regulator
  • • Dirty or damaged burner
  • • Control knob or valve issues

Common replacements: Regulator, burner assembly, control valve

Control Knobs Not Working

Broken or unresponsive knobs can prevent accurate temperature control and safe stove operation.

  • • Cracked or worn control knobs
  • • Faulty selector switches
  • • Internal control damage

Common replacements: Control knob, selector switch
