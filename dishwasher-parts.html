Troubleshooting

Common Dishwasher Problems

When a dishwasher doesn’t work properly, cleaning performance and efficiency suffer. Below are the most common dishwasher issues and the parts that typically fix them.

Dishes Not Getting Clean

Poor cleaning results often occur when water circulation or spray pressure is reduced.

  • • Clogged spray arms
  • • Worn circulation pump
  • • Blocked or dirty filter

Common replacements: Spray arm, circulation pump, filter

Dishwasher Not Draining

Standing water at the bottom of the dishwasher usually indicates a drainage issue.

  • • Faulty drain pump
  • • Clogged drain hose
  • • Blocked check valve

Common replacements: Drain pump, drain hose, check valve

Dishwasher Leaking Water

Leaks can cause floor damage and are often related to worn seals or faulty components.

  • • Damaged door gasket
  • • Loose or cracked water inlet valve
  • • Worn pump seal

Common replacements: Door gasket, inlet valve, pump seal

Dishwasher Won’t Start

When the dishwasher won’t start, the problem is usually electrical or safety-related.

  • • Faulty door latch or switch
  • • Defective control board
  • • Power supply issues

Common replacements: Door latch, control board
